resume

I spend the majority of my time in Photoshop and Illustrator. I’m comfortable with InDesign, and I know a bit about After Effects, too. I work well independently, or collaboratively in a group. My newfound love is product design, but I have strong foundations in branding, editorial, advertising and marketing. Web, apps, email, print—I’ve designed for it all.

November 2012 – Present: I’m a Designer for Thrillist Media Group. I’ve had a hand in every aspect of design throughout the company and been part of a huge creative shift over the past two years. I’ve contributed to site re-designs, app launches, email blasts, numerous infographics, and countless banners and mocks.

Summer 2012: I interned as a Designer at InteliSpend. I developed an enticing campaign and set of marketing materials to promote new products and savings during the company’s busiest quarter of the year. I further developed the ability to take direction from –and effectively work with– the creative director, senior designers, and project managers.

April 2009 – July 2012: I worked at Mizzou Media as a Designer. I managed a group of designers to conceptualize and create a department-wide brand identity through print and social campaigns, contests, and workshops. Fresh designs gave rise to greater interest in our products, resulting in an increase in profitability for the department.

Summer 2011: I interned as a Designer at InteliSpend. I transformed an old and poorly favored identity into one with renewed vigor; heightening employee satisfaction and enjoyment in the workplace. All while building a base-understanding of working in a corporate environment, and following design processes and standards set in place.
